The Elder Futhark: Full Investigation

1. Historical Context

The Elder Futhark is the earliest known runic alphabet, in use from about 150 CE to 800 CE. It spread across the Germanic world, from southern Scandinavia to northern Germany, and even to parts of Eastern Europe.

  • Likely developed from Italic or Latin alphabets, but adapted for Proto-Germanic speech.
  • The name “Futhark” comes from its first six runes: ᚠ (F) – ᚢ (U) – ᚦ (Th) – ᚨ (A) – ᚱ (R) – ᚲ (K).

2. Structure of the Elder Futhark

The alphabet contains 24 runes, divided into three ættir (families) of eight runes each. These divisions may have had ritual or mystical meaning.

  • ᚠᚢᚦᚨᚱᚲᚷᚹ First ætt (Freyr’s Ætt): Associated with wealth, fertility, and beginnings.
  • ᚺᚾᛁᛃᛇᛈᛉᛊ Second ætt (Heimdall’s Ætt): Linked with conflict, trials, and transformation.
  • ᛏᛒᛖᛗᛚᛜᛞᛟ Third ætt (Tyr’s Ætt): Connected to destiny, spirituality, and cosmic forces.

3. The 24 Runes and Their Meanings

First Ætt (ᚠ–ᚷ)

  1. ᚠ Fehu (F) – cattle, wealth, prosperity
  2. ᚢ Uruz (U) – aurochs, primal strength, health
  3. ᚦ Thurisaz (Th) – thorn/giant, danger, chaos, protection
  4. ᚨ Ansuz (A) – Odin/god, wisdom, inspiration
  5. ᚱ Raido (R) – journey, travel, rhythm
  6. ᚲ Kaunan/Kenaz (K/C) – torch, knowledge, illumination
  7. ᚷ Gebo (G) – gift, generosity, partnership
  8. ᚹ Wunjo (W/V) – joy, harmony, success

Second Ætt (ᚺ–ᛉ)

  1. ᚺ Hagalaz (H) – hail, disruption, transformation
  2. ᚾ Naudiz (N) – need, hardship, endurance
  3. ᛁ Isa (I) – ice, stillness, stagnation, clarity
  4. ᛃ Jera (J/Y) – year/harvest, cycles, reward
  5. ᛇ Eiwaz (Ei) – yew tree, death, rebirth, resilience
  6. ᛈ Perthro (P) – mystery, chance, fate, hidden things
  7. ᛉ Algiz (Z/R) – elk, protection, defense, spirituality
  8. ᛊ Sowilo (S) – sun, vitality, success

Third Ætt (ᛏ–ᛟ)

  1. ᛏ Tiwaz (T) – Tyr, justice, honor, sacrifice
  2. ᛒ Berkano (B) – birch tree, birth, fertility, growth
  3. ᛖ Ehwaz (E) – horse, movement, partnership, trust
  4. ᛗ Mannaz (M) – man/human, society, cooperation
  5. ᛚ Laguz (L) – water, intuition, flow, emotion
  6. ᛜ Ingwaz (Ng) – god Ing/Freyr, fertility, stored energy
  7. ᛞ Dagaz (D) – day, breakthrough, awakening
  8. ᛟ Othala (O) – heritage, homeland, inheritance
Summary: The Elder Futhark (150–800 CE) is the original 24-rune alphabet of the Germanic peoples. It was a practical writing system, but also carried deep symbolic and spiritual meaning. Each rune had a sound, a literal meaning (like “cattle” or “sun”), and a symbolic force (wealth, vitality, protection). Archaeological finds confirm its wide use in both daily life and ritual. Its legacy endures in the simplified Younger Futhark and modern cultural revivals.
